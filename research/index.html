Research Topics

  • Molecular Simulations: The use of computational techniques to simulate the behavior of molecules and study their interactions, providing insights into biological and chemical processes.
  • Membrane Binding: Investigating how molecules, such as proteins or drugs, interact with cell membranes, which is crucial for understanding cellular processes.
  • Transport Mechanisms: Analyzing the mechanisms by which molecules move within and between cells, tissues, or biological systems.
  • Vascular Systems: Studying the complex network of blood vessels and vasculatures in biological tissues, often with an emphasis on realistic modeling.
  • Bioinformatics: Applying computational methods to analyze and interpret biological data, including genomics, proteomics, and transcriptomics.
  • Genomics: Examining the complete set of genes within an organism or a specific cell type to understand genetic variations and functions.
  • Phylogenetics: Constructing evolutionary trees or networks to visualize the genetic relationships and evolutionary history of species or genes.
  • Image Processing: Using computational techniques to enhance, analyze, or manipulate images, often applied to scientific imaging data.
  • 3D Reconstruction: Creating three-dimensional models or representations of objects or structures from two-dimensional data, such as images or scans.
  • Computational Algorithms: Developing and implementing mathematical and logical procedures to solve complex problems or analyze data.
  • SARS-CoV-2: Investigating the coronavirus responsible for COVID-19, often focusing on genomics, epidemiology, and transmission dynamics.
  • Drug Delivery Efficiency: Evaluating the effectiveness of delivering therapeutic drugs to target tissues or cells, optimizing treatment outcomes.
  • Antimicrobial Peptides: Designing and studying peptides that have antimicrobial properties, potentially leading to novel treatments for infections.
  • Blood-Brain Barrier: Investigating the barrier that separates the bloodstream from the brain, crucial for understanding drug delivery to the central nervous system.
  • Mathematical Modeling: Developing mathematical models to simulate biological phenomena, analyze experimental data, and make predictions.
  • High-Performance Computing: Utilizing powerful computing resources to perform complex simulations, data analysis, and scientific computations.
  • Data Visualization: Creating graphical representations of data to facilitate understanding and communicate research findings effectively.
  • Automation: Developing scripts and software to automate data analysis, enhance workflow efficiency, and improve data quality.
  • Teaching: Sharing knowledge and expertise with students, often in the fields of computational chemistry and biology.
  • Curriculum Development: Collaborating to create educational content, design courses, and enhance teaching approaches.
